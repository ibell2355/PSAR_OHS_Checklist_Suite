Non-Inflatable PFD Integrity Testing Procedure

Intent

The purpose of this procedure is to establish the importance of and to provide guidance on the periodic integrity testing of Personal Flotation Devices (PFDs), or life jackets, against manufacturer specification for Parkland County Search and Rescue.

Although PFDs do not typically have expiration dates, they do degrade over time. Degradation of PFDs can be caused by many factors such as foam breakdown over time, wear and tear, and exposure to the elements, resulting in a reduction to buoyancy and effectiveness as a safety device.

Process

Frequency

Buoyancy testing and inspection of PFDs should be conducted once a year.

In an October 24, 2019 bulletin from Transport Canada pertaining to the inspection and maintenance of Inflatable Lifejackets and Personal Flotation Devices, they recommend a full inspection be done once a year. We therefore consider it prudent to follow this guidance for non-inflatable PFDs as well.

Materials and Equipment Required

The following materials and equipment will be needed to perform a buoyancy test.

  • 1x A large container with a wide top opening capable of holding sufficient water to fully submerge the PFD and weight without it touching the walls or bottom of the container. For example, a 55 Gallon barrel.
  • 1x 10 kg weight that can be attached with rope to the PFD.
  • 1x luggage scale. This should be accurate and properly calibrated.
  • 2x length of rope to attach the weight to the PFD and the PFD to the luggage scale without adding significant bulk or weight. For example, a few feet of parachute cord. Carabiners may also work for this.
  • 1x Tracking sheet to record result. For example, a printed sheet (example attached) or a computer spreadsheet.
  • 1x Permanent marker.
  • Water source to fill the container.
  • 2x volunteers. One to complete the test and one to record results.
  • All PFDs that require testing.

Steps

Each PFD should be inspected visually to ensure all of the following are true:

  • There is no visual damage cuts, rips, tears, or holes in any part of the PFD or straps.
  • There are no other significant signs of wear and tear.
  • All straps, buckles, and zippers are in good working order and securely attached.

The following steps should be followed to complete a buoyancy test of each PFD. Test can be performed indoors or outdoors:

  1. Prepare for the test.
    1. This will include sorting PFDs and ensuring each has a unique identifier. This will ensure results can be traced back to the specific device and allow for tracking of device performance over time.
    2. Fill the water container with clean fresh water ensuring nothing is added that could impact buoyancy forces.
  2. Record baseline measurement
    1. Attach the 10kg weight directly to the luggage scale using the rope. No PFD should be attached for this test.
    2. Lower the weight into the water until fully submerged.
    3. Record the new reading from the scale with buoyancy forces acting on the weight. This is your “Baseline Weight” and should be less than the initial reading of ~10 kg. You only need to record the Baseline Weight once at the start of the test.
  3. Begin testing PFDs
    1. Select a PFD and record its unique identifier, manufacturer’s specifications, and any other details required on the tracking sheet.
    2. Attach the first PFD to the luggage scale and the weight to the bottom of the PFD using the rope.
    3. Lower the weight and PFD into the water container until fully submerged.
    4. Allow time for any air bubbles being released to stop.
    5. Important: Ensure the PFD and the weight are not touching any part of the container including the sides and bottom. The weight and PFD should be independently suspended by the scale.
    6. Record the resulting reading from the scale in the appropriate spot on the tracking sheet. This is your “Test Weight”.
  4. Calculating the result
    1. Subtract the Test Weight from the Baseline Weight to determine the buoyancy force acting on the weight from the PFD in kg.
    2. Multiply the result by 9.81, representing the force conversion from Kilograms (kg) to Newtons (N).
    3. Record the result on the tracking sheet.
  5. Compare the result to the manufacturer’s specifications.
    1. The resulting force in N should meet or exceed the specification provided by the manufacturer.
    2. Any PFDs that fail to meet or exceed the manufacturer specification should be marked using a permanent marker with “Do Not Use” and disposed of or separated from the others immediately for disposal later.
    3. It is recommended this process be followed completely for each PFD before starting the test for the next one to ensure results are not mixed up.

Time to complete the process will depend on the number of PFDs.

Responsibilities

The OHS Officer or designate is responsible for coordinating and ensuring annual PFD integrity testing is completed and results are recorded.
